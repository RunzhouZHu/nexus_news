How does the user navigate connections?

When someone taps a news event, how do related stories surface around it?

A — Interactive Knowledge Graph

Events are nodes in a zoomable, pannable graph. Click any node to re-center on it. Color-coded edges show relationship type (cause, effect, context). Feels like exploring a live map.

CAUSE — Jan 12
OPEC announces output cuts
EVENT — Feb 3
⚡ Gas prices spike 18%
EFFECT — Feb 5
Airlines cancel 300 routes
EFFECT — Feb 9
Food delivery prices rise

B — Vertical Timeline Chain

A scrollable, chronological story. The selected event is the anchor; causes appear above it, effects below. Simple and linear — easy to follow but harder to show branching complexity.

READING
Gas Prices Spike 18% in February
Global fuel markets reacted sharply after OPEC announced...
📎 3 causes ⚡ 5 effects 🔗 2 related
CONNECTED STORIES
OPEC cuts output by 2M barrels
Airlines cancel 300 routes globally
Food delivery prices up 12%
Consumer confidence drops

C — Article + Connected Panel

Users read a news article normally. A side/bottom panel shows connected stories grouped by type (causes, effects, related). Familiar reading experience with discovery baked in.
