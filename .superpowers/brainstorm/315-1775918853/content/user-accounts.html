Do users have accounts and personalization?

This determines whether the app is a passive reading tool or an active platform users return to.

A

No Accounts — Public Explorer

Anyone opens the app and immediately starts exploring the graph. No sign-up friction. Like Wikipedia — fully public, read-only for casual users. Simpler to build and faster to launch.

B

Optional Accounts — Save & Follow

Browse freely without an account. Sign up to: save graph explorations, follow topics or events, get notifications when connected news breaks. Accounts unlock personalization without blocking access.

C

Full Accounts — Contribute & Curate

Logged-in users can submit news events, propose connections, upvote/downvote AI-suggested links, and leave reactions. The community actively shapes the graph. More powerful, but requires trust and moderation systems.
