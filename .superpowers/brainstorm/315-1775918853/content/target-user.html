Who is the primary user of this app?

Understanding your audience shapes everything — how connections are made, how complex the graph can be, and how it's presented.

A

General Public / Curious Readers

Everyday people who want to understand why something happened — tracing events back to their roots without needing expertise. Think: "Why did gas prices spike?"

B

Journalists & Researchers

Professionals who need to map complex stories — tracking how a political scandal, crisis, or trend evolved across sources and time. Deep linking, citations, and export matter here.

C

Students & Educators

People learning about history or current events, where cause-and-effect chains are a teaching tool. Structured timelines, context, and simplicity are key.

D

Analysts & Decision Makers

Business, policy, or finance professionals tracking how world events ripple into their domain. Data density, filtering, and integration with other tools matter most.
